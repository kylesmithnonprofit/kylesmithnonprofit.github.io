excerpt: 'We examine the impact of Internal Revenue Service (IRS) code section 4960 of the Tax Cuts and Jobs Act of 2017 (TCJA) on nonprofit organizations (NPOs). This section imposes a 21% excise tax on nonprofit employee compensation exceeding $1 million per covered individual. As this is an exogenous shock imposing a cost on NPOs with highly paid employees, it leads us to examine whether those employees shared the newly added cost via a reduction in their compensation. Using a difference-in-differences analysis on IRS Form 990 data for nearly 40,000 nonprofit employee-year observations from 2015 to 2020, we fail to find evidence that the level of compensation for treated employees decreased, but do find a lower growth rate for treated employee compensation post-section 4960. These results are consistent with highly paid employees being reluctant, on average, to take a pay cut, but more willing to accept a reduction in their rate of pay growth. Our results are robust to alternative treatment specifications and control samples, such as employees earning more than $1 million but not covered under section 4960, and medical professionals specifically exempt from section 4960. We also find that compensation decreases are more likely for treated employees post-4960, and that replacements for treated CEOs take an even steeper pay cut post-4960. Additionally, we observe increased turnover for treated CEOs post-4960, consistent with section 4960 leading to conflicts between treated CEOs and their boards regarding the excise tax and who should bear its cost.'
