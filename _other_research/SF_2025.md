excerpt: 'This article came from an observation I made when conducting a qualitative study: many private foundations are giving away tens/hundreds of millions of dollars a year with just a few staff. Going to the data, I found that 69% of private foundations report no wage expense at all! These PF made an aggregate $11 billion in grants last year. We also compute two measures: the median foundation paid $0.08 in wage expense per $1 given away and $1,505 in wage expense per grant decision. It is difficult in the nonprofit sector to make a case for more administrative expense. And it is tempting to ask: "how hard can it be to give away money?". But we argue that for foundations to embody the ideals of accountability, return on investment, equality of opportunity, and trust-based philanthropy, it is important to assess if they have adequate staff.'
