excerpt: 'This chapter provides a high-level overview of the usage of behavioral methods in nonprofit accounting research. We begin by defining the scope of behavioral methods used in the nonprofit accounting literature: experiments, surveys, and qualitative methods. We then discuss the advantages of experiments, surveys, and qualitative methods. Within this scope, we identify 194 papers published in a selection of 30 journals from 2000-2021. We discuss key research topics and findings of behavioral nonprofit accounting research and conclude with numerous suggestions for future research. Overall, we argue that behavioral methods are underutilized and offer substantial opportunities for future nonprofit accounting research.'
